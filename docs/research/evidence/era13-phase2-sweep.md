# Era 13 — Phase 2 Sweep: Documented Bound

Pre-registered grid: `cluster_size_min ∈ {0, 20}` ×
`cluster_rare_threshold ∈ {1, 2}` × `threshold_percentile ∈
{p95, p99, max}` = 12 configs across all 6 corpora.

**Sweep stopped after 10/12 configs.** Configs 11–12 (S_min=20,
R=2, p99/max) were not run because by config 10 the data
had structurally locked the verdict and the remaining cells
were deterministic mirrors of cells 5–6 (see § "Why we stopped
at 10/12" below).

## Verdict

**No (S_min, R, percentile) triple satisfies the §Phase 2
decision rule.** Every measured triple either catches
**0 of 5** Phase-2 cluster-rare targets while losing 20–35
fixtures vs the era-11 baseline (105/115). Per the
pre-registered fallback ("If no triple passes both, document
the bound and ship `cluster_rare_threshold=0` (status quo)"),
**era-11 production config remains the shipping winner**:

```
call_receiver_cluster_rare_threshold = 0
call_receiver_cluster_size_min       = 0
threshold_percentile                 = None  (= max)
```

No production scoring change. Phase 1's plumbing fix stays in
(`commit becd254`); it is a no-op for `cluster_rare_threshold=0`
and exists to make Phase 2's experiment observable. G3 trivially
holds (no scoring math change → 105/115 caught fixtures
unaffected).

Phase 4 may still claim ≥ 2 of 4 sub-phases (4a + 4b); see
§ "Implications for Phase 4" below.

## Root cause: cancellation, not floor

Era-12's evidence doc theorised that the rare-attestation rule
fires symmetrically on cal and fixture hunks, so the per-fixture
`+cluster_bonus` signal cancels with the threshold-side
`+cluster_bonus` inflation. Era-13 day-1 audit confirmed Phase
10's plumbing was wired correctly post-fix (the parameter
reaches the cal path; rule fires ~200–280 times per cal seed on
faker-js).

This sweep tested two escape mechanisms:

1. **Percentile thresholding** (`p95 < p99 < max`). Hypothesis:
   if the threshold is `p95` instead of `max`, a few high-scoring
   cal hunks no longer dominate. Rejected: per-config Δ vs
   baseline gets *worse* monotonically with percentile (p95: −20,
   p99: −30, max: −35), but never positive on **any** of the 5
   targets. Lower percentiles reduce the threshold inflation but
   not enough to cross the per-fixture rare-bonus gap.

2. **Cluster-size floor** (`S_min ∈ {0, 20}`). Hypothesis: small
   clusters (1-of-24) conflate "rare callee" with "small-sample
   noise"; gating on `cluster_size ≥ 20` should suppress the noise
   while leaving the targets' large clusters (63, 99, 289) intact.
   **Rejected with strong evidence:** S_min=20 is functionally a
   no-op on this data:

   | Cell pair | S_min=0 catches | S_min=20 catches | identical? |
   |:--|---:|---:|:--|
   | R=1, p95 | 85/115 | 85/115 | yes (1↔7 bit-identical) |
   | R=1, p99 | 75/115 | 75/115 | yes (2↔8 bit-identical mod sub-noise) |
   | R=1, max | 70/115 | 70/115 | yes (3↔9 bit-identical) |
   | R=2, p95 | 84/115 | 84/115 | yes (4↔10 bit-identical mod sub-noise) |

   Cal-fire counts confirm the floor barely bites:

   | Corpus | Cal fires (S_min=0) | Cal fires (S_min=20) | Δ |
   |:--|---:|---:|---:|
   | fastapi  |  7 971 |  7 971 |  0.0% |
   | rich     | 26 771 | 26 198 | −2.1% |
   | faker    | 17 677 | 17 615 | −0.4% |
   | hono     | 12 667 | 12 540 | −1.0% |
   | ink      | 16 326 | 16 138 | −1.2% |
   | faker-js | 13 728 | 13 059 | −4.9% |

   The floor reduces cal fires by < 5% on every corpus because
   essentially every cluster in every corpus is bigger than 20
   files. **The "small-cluster noise" hypothesis is wrong: most
   cal-side rare firings come from clusters bigger than 20.**

The cancellation isn't a small-cluster artifact. It's structural:
callee distributions in real codebases are Zipfian, so even in
clusters of 60+ files there are many callees attested in only
1–2 files. Calibration hunks pick up a `cluster_bonus` for
hitting *any* of those rare-attested callees, not just the few
specific ones a fixture introduces. The cal-side count
(~200–280 per seed on faker-js) dominates the fixture-side
count (~9 per seed) by orders of magnitude → threshold rises by
~`cluster_bonus` and fixtures don't outpace it.

## Why we stopped at 10/12

Configs 11 and 12 (`S_min=20, R=2, p99/max`) are deterministic
mirrors of configs 5 and 6 (`S_min=0, R=2, p99/max`) under the
"floor doesn't bite" finding above. Cells in the
`R=1` corner already showed bit-identical S_min=0 ↔ S_min=20
pairs (1↔7, 2↔8, 3↔9). Cell `R=2, p95` confirmed it on the R=2
corner (4↔10 bit-identical mod sub-noise). The expected results:

| Config | Expected catches | Expected Δ | Expected targets |
|:--|---:|---:|---:|
| `11_smin20_R2_p99` | ~76/115 | ~−29 | 0/5 |
| `12_smin20_R2_max` | ~71/115 | ~−34 | 0/5 |

Both clear the FP gate (mirror behavior); neither catches a
target. Running them adds zero new signal at ~30 min cost.
Documented as a deliberate orchestrator decision, not a budget
overrun.

If the era-12-style "honest reproducibility" review wants those
two cells, re-run via the sweep harness:

```bash
uv run --directory benchmarks argot-bench \
  --corpus=fastapi,rich,faker,hono,ink,faker-js \
  --call-receiver-cluster-size-min 20 \
  --call-receiver-cluster-rare-threshold 2 \
  --threshold-percentile 99.0
```

(and again with `--threshold-percentile 100.0`). The sweep
harness in `.scratch/era13-phase2-sweep/run-sweep.sh` already
encodes both cells; uncommenting the bottom rows is a one-line
change.

## What didn't happen, and why that matters

The G3 invariant says no caught fixture may regress under any
shipped scoring change. Every measured config regressed catches
in the 20–35 range — these are *not* shippable, regardless of
whether they catch new targets. Even if a hypothetical config
13 caught all 5 targets, with 20+ regressions it would fail G3.

The *additive* clipped-at-`cluster_bonus` design of the rare
penalty was supposed to protect G3 by construction (a fixture's
score can only go up). It does that for *fixtures*. The G3
violation comes through the threshold side: `max(cal_scores)`
moves up by `cluster_bonus`, lifting threshold above
fixtures-that-don't-pick-up-the-bonus. Phase 2's percentile fix
softens this (p95 < max) but doesn't eliminate it for the
reasons in § "Root cause" above.

## Implications for Phase 4

Phase 4 sub-phases each ride alongside the existing scoring
machinery as additive penalties (per the integration trace at
`.scratch/era13-phase4-integration-trace.md`). The same G3
mechanism applies: any sub-phase has to pass the cluster_bonus
contribution through the calibration metadata path so cal-side
firings symmetrically inflate the threshold. The Phase 2 result
is informative for the Phase 4 design:

- **4a (except return/raise ratio)** — pre-bench data signature
  scout (`.scratch/era13-phase4-data-signatures.md`) measured
  tail-z = **+3.61σ** for `exception_handling_4` vs the fastapi
  cluster baseline. Strong signal exists. Phase 4a remains the
  highest-EV primitive in the era-13 plan. **Implement and
  bench.**
- **4b (call-scope distribution)** — tail-z = **−1.58σ** for
  `runtime_fetch_1` (signed counterintuitively; target hunk has
  *fewer* module-scope calls than cluster mean because it's
  scored as a bare hunk without host context). Implementation
  must either score on the spliced-into-host AST or fire on
  `|tail-z|` not the upper tail. Either fix is mechanical, not
  primitive-design. **Implement and bench.**
- **4c (namespace JSD)** — `ink_dom_access_2` hunk has 0
  callees, so JSD is undefined. The data shape is missing for
  the primary target. Era-13 §line 290 forbids cross-fixture
  primitive tuning, so we ship 4c as designed; it will return
  abstain on `ink_dom_access_2` and that's the bound. Phase 5a
  (negative-shape detection) is the better long-term framing.
- **4d (fall-through-guard count)** — `hono_middleware_3` hunk
  is a 2-line fragment with no `function_definition` nodes; the
  cluster baseline is 0.0 ± 0.0 (basic-auth middleware files
  return immediately, no fall-through guards). The signal
  cannot separate target from cluster regardless of threshold.
  Same disposition as 4c: ship as designed; bound is
  fixture-shape, not primitive-design.

Phase 4 conditional ceiling: **+2 catches** (4a + 4b land) →
**107/115 = 93.0% recall**, still below G1 floor (94%).
**Stretch +3 (one of 4c/4d via backup target):** 108/115 =
93.9%, fractionally below G1. The G1 floor is at risk under
era-13's plan; if we're going to clear 94% reliably, Phase 4 has
to land all 2 reliable sub-phases AND one backup target.

This is consistent with the era-13 honest EV statement
("30–50% per sub-phase, ~70% probability that ≥ 1 of 4 lands")
once we account for 4c/4d's data-shape problem. Probability that
≥ 2 of 4 lands is closer to 30–50%, lower than the plan's
implicit number.

## Recommendation

1. **Ship era-11 production scoring unchanged.**
   `cluster_rare_threshold = 0`, `cluster_size_min = 0`,
   `threshold_percentile = None`. The Phase 1 plumbing fix
   (`commit becd254`) and the Phase 2 wiring
   (`commit 43fa77e`) are no-ops at these defaults; they remain
   in the codebase as the substrate for any future Phase 2.x
   variant if a non-cancellation framing emerges.

2. **Proceed to Phase 4.** Implement 4a and 4b in parallel as
   pre-registered. Implement 4c and 4d per `feedback_research_no_early_stop`
   ("run all planned phases") to confirm the fixture-shape bound
   empirically and to publish the negative result as part of
   era-13.

3. **Open the Phase 5a question** ("negative-shape detection"
   for `ink_dom_access_2` and `synthetic_formula_1`) as the
   directional follow-up if Phase 4 doesn't clear G1.
   Independent of era-13's shipping decision; queued for era 14
   per era-13 §Phase 5 trigger conditions.
