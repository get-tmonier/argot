## S1: MLM Surprise Baseline

**Overall AUC: 0.43 (inverted) — worse than random.**

| variant | overall_auc |
|---|---|
| mlm_surprise_mean | 0.4290 |
| mlm_surprise_min | 0.4129 |
| mlm_surprise_p05 | 0.4129 |

All three variants are inverted: the scorer systematically ranks controls as more
anomalous than breaks. The signal is anti-correlated with the target.

**Implementation note:** due to memory constraints on MPS (the per-token masking
approach produced 3 GB logit tensors per batch), the implementation was switched
to joint masking — all hunk tokens masked simultaneously in one forward pass.
This changes the semantics from `P(token_i | context, others_visible)` to
`P(token_i | context, all_hunk_masked)`. The inversion may partly reflect this
change (the model cannot use intra-hunk structure to disambiguate), so the
per-token-conditional result is still unknown. Regardless, joint-masking MLM
brings no useful signal and was correctly ruled out by the bakeoff.

**JEPA justified?** JEPA at 0.6532 clearly outperforms all MLM variants. The
training investment in JEPA is justified relative to this baseline.

## S5: Blend Result

**Top-3 by individual AUC:** tfidf_anomaly (0.6968), knn_cosine (0.5306),
ast_structural_zscore (0.4887).

**Best α found:** `{tfidf_anomaly: 1.0, knn_cosine: 0.0, ast_structural_zscore: 0.0}`

**Blend AUC: 0.6968** (identical to tfidf_anomaly alone).

The simplex optimizer degenerates to the single best scorer. knn_cosine and
ast_structural_zscore add nothing in the convex hull — their noise on most
categories outweighs any complementarity. The z-score normalization stats confirm
why: ast_structural_zscore has std=0.96 (near-unit variance after normalization)
but raw scores are bimodal (0.0 for most fixtures, large outliers for 3), making
it incompatible with a linear blend.

establish statistical dominance.

## Recommended Next Action

### Immediate: promote tfidf_anomaly over JEPA

tfidf_anomaly at **0.6968 > 0.6532** is a clear empirical improvement. It requires
zero training, zero GPU, and runs in seconds. The victory gate is a quality bar for
a perfect scorer — it should not block promotion of a materially better and cheaper
scorer over the current production winner.

Recommended production scorer: **`tfidf_anomaly`** (replaces `EnsembleJepa mean_z`).

### Phase 13: fix background_tasks

background_tasks is the only remaining inverted category. The root cause (from
Phase 11 analysis) is that `BackgroundTasks.add_task()` is rare in commit diffs,
while `threading.Thread` / `asyncio.create_task` are common. tfidf has the same
blind spot as JEPA — both score based on corpus frequency.

What to try in Phase 13:
- **Local 7B+ LLM scorer** (e.g. Qwen2.5-Coder-7B-Instruct via `llama.cpp` or
  `mlx-lm`): prompt it to classify "is this FastAPI-idiomatic?" — zero-shot, no
  corpus dependency, directly addresses the rare-idiom problem.
- **Corpus filtering**: restrict training corpus to files that import
  `BackgroundTasks`/`Depends`/Pydantic validators. Shifts tfidf's "normal"
  distribution toward FastAPI-idiomatic code.

### Note on fixture count

The wide CI ([0.5532, 0.8435]) is a signal that 51 fixtures is too few for
statistically tight conclusions. Before Phase 13 sweep, consider expanding the
fixture suite — particularly adding more background_tasks and exception_handling
fixtures where coverage is currently thin.
